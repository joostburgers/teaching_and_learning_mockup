Preface

This activity is meant to help orient your students to “where in the world” they are as they read. This lesson is to be used before reading the novel and is useful in highlighting aesthetic impact (through the cultural perspective/setting) and the author’s structural choices (through the narrative structure). The digital escape room became popular in the age of COVID, but this lesson is adaptable for a physical escape room, as well. If you are unfamiliar with digital escape rooms, please check out the "Resources" section for more information!

Instructions

Create Groups

Break students into groups of three and have each student take on a role: researcher, technician, recorder.

  • The researcher is in charge of maneuvering the group through the DY website.
  • The technician keeps the Escape Room form up and inputs the codes/responses.
  • The recorder keeps track of all the answers and locations of answers in one document so they can be referenced later.

Customize Google Form

Students work through the escape room Google Form. This link generates a copy that can be customized to suit the particular class context.

Tip: Students will need a personal device (cellphones are not recommended for the purpose of navigating the DY website, but can be used for the Google Form or record keeping)

Final Product

Give students the following prompt for reflection:

Hypothesize how Faulkner's narrative structure is going to influence our understanding of the events in the story. Consider the characters, their ages, their genders, etc. What is the difference between narrative perspective and narrative point of view? Students can either submit this as an informal assessment or jot down notes to share in a class discussion.
