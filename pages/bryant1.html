Preface

This lesson functions as a primer before reading the novel. It highlights the text’s aesthetics through the cultural perspective/setting and the narrative structure. It also helps students orient themselves in the text as they start their reading.

The digital escape room became popular in the age of COVID, but this lesson is adaptable for a physical escape room as well. If you are unfamiliar with digital escape rooms, please check out the “Resources” section for more information!

Activities

1. Escape Room

Explore: Group Work

Overview

Students will work in groups to complete a digital escape room using a Google Form.

A copy of this form can be downloaded and modified to meet individual classroom needs.

  • Google Form

Tip: Students will need a personal device to complete this task.

Cellphones are not recommended for the purpose of navigating the DY website, but can be used for the Google Form or record keeping

Instructions
  1. Break students into groups of three
  2. Each student takes on a role: researcher, technician, or recorder.
    1. Researcher maneuvers the group through the DY website.
    2. Technician keeps the Escape Room form up and inputs the codes/responses.
    3. Recorder keeps track of all the answers and locations of answers in one document so they can be referenced later.

Final Product

Consider following prompt for reflection:

Hypothesize how Faulkner’s narrative structure is going to influence our understanding of the events in the story. Consider the characters, their ages, their genders, etc. What is the difference between narrative perspective and narrative point of view? Students can either submit this as an informal assessment or jot down notes to share in a class discussion.
