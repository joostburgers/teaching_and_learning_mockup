Preface

For this assignment students should have a grasp of the concepts of theme and motif.

Throughout these exercises, students will track a theme in As I Lay Dying using quotes that are listed in Digital Yoknapatawpha. They will keep track of these quotes and add quotes of their own that can be added to the database. This will help students who are new to Faulkner grasp a trend in the novel and analyze it through a literary analysis essay which will be composed at the end of the unit.

In such a difficult novel, the database, journal, and essay will make Faulkner more accessible to students who are not as well versed in Faulknerian writing.

Activities

1. Journal Entries on the Novel

Explore

Walkthrough: Event Keyword Search

The Event Keyword Search allows users to retrieve all of the events across a single text or all texts that contain a specific keyword.

These keywords have been manually entered by Faulkner scholars, and describe concepts that cannot be found through a simple word search. For example, the word “absence” never occurs in As I Lay Dying, but the theme of absence/loss occurs 28 times.

Instructions
  1. From the DY Main Menu, choose:
    • Search
    • Events
  2. Set Text as As I Lay Dying
  3. Scroll the “Themes & Motifs” selections.
    • There are many options available. Not all themes and motifs are available for every text one might select.
  4. Select “Absence/Loss”
  5. Click “Search”

Note: Page numbers list where that particular theme can be found.

All keywords are works of interpretation and therefore keywords may occur on pages not listed on DY. Therefore, you should be diligent in looking for themes and motifs as you read the novel.

Respond: Journal Entries for As I Lay Dying

Choose one of the following themes in DY that you would like to track. You may only choose one of these four:
  • Absence/Loss
  • Chaos/Order
  • Character
  • Meaning
Once you have chosen your theme, search for it in DY using the steps previously provided in the "Explore" section.
  1. Record the page numbers and quotes where this theme is present according to DY.
    • All quotes noted in DY are required in your journal.
    • This is where your journal starts.
  2. As you read the novel, you will expand the initial set of quotes by recording additional quotes every time you think that your theme is present. For each quote provide:
    1. Character(s)
    2. Page number(s)
    3. Brief analysis
  3. You should also record a minimum of 5 of your own quotes not listed in DY.
    • Make sure you are saving your commentary for each quote until you have reached that point of the novel.
    • Avoid analyzing quotes you have not read in context.

Final Product

  1. Review the journal you kept while reading As I Lay Dying. Note any particular trends and motifs.
  2. Write an essay in which you discuss what motif(s) reinforce the theme you tracked in your journal.
  3. Review a peer’s essay using the essay rubric.
