Preface

This series of assignments is to be completed once students have grasped the concepts of theme and motif. Throughout these exercises, students will track a theme in As I Lay Dying using quotes that are listed in Digital Yoknapatawpha. They will keep track of these quotes and add quotes of their own that can be added to the database. This will help students who are new to Faulkner grasp a trend in the novel and analyze it through a literary analysis essay which will be composed at the end of the unit. In such a difficult novel, the database, journal, and essay will make Faulkner more accessible to students who are not as well versed in Faulknerian writing.

Activities

Activity #1

Explore
Walkthrough: Event Keyword Search
The Event Keyword Search allows users to retrieve all of the events across a single text or all texts that contain a specific keyword. These keywords have been manually entered by Faulkner scholars, and describe concepts that cannot be found through a simple word search. For example, the word absence never occurs in As I Lay Dying, but the theme of absence/loss occurs 28 times.
  • From the navigation bar on the Digital Yoknapatawpha Website, click "Search"
  • Select Events
  • Set Text As I Lay Dying
  • Scroll the “Themes & Motifs” selections.
    • There are many options available. Not all themes and motifs are available for every text one might select.
  • Select “Absence/Loss.”
  • Click "Search"

Note: Page numbers are listed where that particular theme can be found. All keywords are works of interpretation and therefore it is possible that the keyword occurs on pages not listed on DY. Therefore, you should be diligent in looking for themes and motifs as you read the novel.

Study: As I Lay Daying
Instructions
  • Now, you will begin reading As I Lay Dying.
Respond
Please complete:
  • Journal entries as you read As I Lay Dying. You will turn these in for a grade.

Final Product: Motif Essay and Peer Review

  1. Now that you have tracked a theme through the novel, compose an essay on motifs present in the novel.
  2. Review a peer’s essay using the essay rubric.
