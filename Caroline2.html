Explore: Narrative Analysis

Walkthrough: About the text tool

The about the text tool uses outside research to help further push the understanding of the narrative as well as Faulkner's purpose in writing the narrative.

  1. Navigate to the Digital Yoknapatawpha home page
  2. Select “Lion” from the bookshelf
    • This will open the main map display for “Lion”
  3. After selecting the Lion, the page will appear with the map, display control panel, title with the contributing authors, timeline, and page correlation.
  4. Hover your cursor over the characters on the map to reveal their name
  5. Select the title with the contributing author in the left bottom corner, and the about the text should appear.

    6. Using the About the Text section, use your best interpretation of the information to answer the following questions.

    • What could Faulkner's numerous rewrites from different perspectives give more insight to without reading it?
    • How does having an outside view differ from being there in the moment?
    • How is the vagueness of the dating of the narrative significant to the message Faulkner is trying to get across?
    • Holly Knoll- what does the imagery of Lion’s grave being washed away evoke in the reader?
Instructions
  1. From the DY Main Menu choose:
    • Visualizations
    • Narrative Analysis
  2. Select “Lion” from the drop-down menu on the left.
  3. Hover over each chart to see more detailed information about each data point
  4. Click “Add Chart”
  5. Select “Lion” from the new drop-down menu
  6. Click “Date Order” on one of the charts to compare the different time structures
Overview

Digital Yoknapatawpha’s tools allow users to generate visual representations of social relations in each text, which you will utilize to think critically about the representations of race, class, and gender in Faulkner’s fiction.

This activity produces visualizations illustrating the story’s centering of white upper-class characters and relative marginalization of working class and Black characters. The activity is to encourage you to better understand the limits of Faulkner’s fiction as historical representation.

Explore: Character Connections

Walkthrough: Character-Character Force Directed Graph

This graph shows how often characters are present or mentioned in the same event. A red line () between two blue boxes () shows at least one interaction, and a thicker line indicates multiple interactions. Characters with more connections are closer to the center, but, importantly, the position of characters are not fixed.

Characters who appear close together in one graph may appear far apart in another. The graph is interactive, so you can click on a character’s name to see their connections. You can also click and drag characters to reposition them.

Instructions
  1. Click "Visualization" on the navigation bar at the top of the Digital Yoknapatawpha homepage
    • Click Character-Character
  2. Set Text as “Lion”
  3. Complete the following searches and answer the related questions:
    1. All characters
      • Set Text as “A Rose for Emily”
      • Leave all other fields blank
      • Click Search
    2. Race
      • Set Race as “White”
      • Click Search
      • Repeat for each race
      • Switch Rank between “Major” and “Major and Secondary”
        Questions
        • Approximately how many total characters appear as white?
        • How many total characters appear as Black?
        • Which major and secondary characters appear as white?
        • Which major and secondary characters appear as Black?
    3. Gender
      • Set Gender as “Male”
      • Click Search
      • Repeat for each gender
        Questions
        • About how many male characters appear in the story, and how are they connected?
        • About how many female characters appear in the story, and how are they connected?
    4. Class
      • Set Class as “Upper Class”
      • Click Search
      • Repeat for each class
        Questions
        • Which characters appear according to each social class designation: upper class, middle class, lower class, free Black?
    5. Other
      • Select any remaining variables
      • Click Search

        Use the Location-Character Graph to help visual and elaborate on the following questions.

        • What does the lack of women in the story demonstrate to the readers
        • What role does masculinity place in the narrative?
        • How does the lower class versus upper class stereotype lead the others in their interpretation of one another?
        • Does different classes' perspectives allow different views on the death of Lion?

Explore: Character Search

Overview: Digital Yoknapatawpha Map Display Character Search

The map display allows users to pull up records for every location, character, and event in the story. These records contain an overview of each character, the events where they are present and mentioned, and the locations where those events take place.

Instructions
  1. Navigate to the Digital Yoknapatawpha home page
  2. Select “Lion” from the bookshelf
    • This will open the main map display for “Lion”
  3. Select “All” or “Major and Secondary” under “Show Characters” on Display Controls
  4. Hover your cursor over the characters on the map to reveal their name
  5. Select a character for your fan fic by clicking on it
    • For a more targeted search, check the “Alphabetical List” box under “Show Characters”
    • Check the name of a specific character
    • Click “Show”
  6. Click on your chosen character. An informational box will open with the following tabs: Biography, Information, Events, Location. Use the information from this box to complete the questions on the attached character chart.

    7. Using the map, work together in groups to answer the following questions with quotes to support your claims. Present your answers and evidence to the class.

    • What is the significance of the majority of the plot taking place outside of the main town?
    • Boon- how does Boon being perceived as mixed race affect how his actions are interpreted by the others?
    • Hunting camp- what sense of community does this camp inspire? Why is it significant that Lion’s final hours were there?
    • Holly Knoll- what does the imagery of Lion’s grave being washed away evoke in the reader?
